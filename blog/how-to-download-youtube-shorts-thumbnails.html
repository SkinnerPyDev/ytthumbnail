Tips for Creating Better Shorts Thumbnails

  • Use bold text - Shorts thumbnails appear small in the feed. Make any text large, bold, and limited to 2-3 words maximum. Sans-serif fonts like Impact or Montserrat work best at small sizes.
  • Show faces with expression - Thumbnails with human faces showing strong emotions (surprise, excitement, curiosity) consistently get higher click-through rates across all YouTube formats.
  • Bright, saturated colors - Stand out in the vertical Shorts feed with vibrant, contrasting colors. Avoid white or pastel backgrounds that blend into YouTube's interface.
  • Keep it simple - One clear subject or message works significantly better than cluttered designs. Viewers scroll through the Shorts feed quickly and make split-second click decisions.
  • Study competitors - Download thumbnails from the top-performing Shorts in your niche and analyze what visual patterns they share. Look for common color palettes, text styles, and compositions.
  • Maintain brand consistency - Use the same 2-3 brand colors and font across all your Shorts thumbnails. This builds channel branding recognition so viewers start clicking your content by habit.
